Response to Reviewer 2 (Round 3)

We thank the reviewer for the careful reading. The three remaining concerns are addressed below. Each response states the change and the supporting evidence; all numbers come from a full reproduction of the pipeline on NIH ChestX-ray14 and MIMIC-CXR, on both the released TorchXRayVision DenseNet-121 and an independently trained weighted-BCE model, with non-parametric bootstrap confidence intervals.

Concern 1 — OOD / domain-shift and risk-coverage evaluation

"The inter-source evaluation is a proxy; a formal OOD assessment and complete risk-coverage analysis are missing, and the manuscript itself defers these to future work."

Resolution. Both were added, and the contradiction the reviewer implied (the contribution claimed domain-shift robustness while the limitations disclaimed it) is removed.

  • Formal leave-one-dataset-out (LODO). Following Cohen et al. (2020), a single-source model is evaluated on a different source. The NIH-trained model on MIMIC shows a clear shift penalty: Cardiomegaly AUC 0.864→0.685, Edema 0.867→0.795, Consolidation 0.793→0.670 (new Table 3).
  • Complete risk-coverage analysis. Per-pathology risk-coverage curves and AURC are now reported (new figures), the analysis previously only described.
  • Contradiction removed. Contribution (3) and the limitations section are reconciled; the future-work disclaimer of OOD is deleted.

New citations: Zech et al. 2018 (hospital confounding), Cohen et al. 2020 (cross-domain limits), Ovadia et al. 2019 (uncertainty under shift).

Concern 2 — rejection mechanism ambiguity, interval method, equations

"Image-level vs label-level inconsistency; the interval method is not defined for deterministic outputs; equations are incomplete."

  • Per-label rejection only. The image-level "accept if at least one class is confident" wording is deleted throughout. Each of the four predictions is independently accepted or deferred; rejection rates are reported per class (Section 3.4, single consistent definition).
  • Equations restored. Binary entropy H(p) = −p log₂ p − (1−p) log₂(1−p), the rule H(p_c) > τ_c, and the boundary are written explicitly (the source file had dropped these objects).
  • Interval method clarified by direct analysis. On a deterministic backbone, the interval-straddles-boundary rule with fixed half-width is rank-identical to entropy thresholding (both monotone in |p−0.5|): risk-coverage AURC under the two scores agrees to within 10⁻⁹ for all four pathologies. We therefore present a single entropy-based rule rather than two described as complementary, and state this explicitly rather than leaving the interval undefined.

Concern 3 — interpretation stronger than evidence; Grad-CAM; reproducibility

"Rejection does not consistently improve AUC (it decreases for NIH / Consolidation at high rejection); broad reliability claims should be moderated; gradient-volatility does not replace Grad-CAM; reproducibility details are missing."

The reviewer is correct, and the results are rebuilt around the metric the evidence supports.

  • AUC claim corrected. With bootstrap CIs, entropy rejection does not improve AUC at any rejection rate, in-distribution (NIH n=22,000: 0 of 20 class×rate cells with a CI excluding 0) or under domain shift (significantly negative), and it significantly decreases AUC for Consolidation (−0.047 at 25% rejection, 95% CI [−0.058, −0.034]). Rejecting near-boundary cases removes the informative middle of the ranking, so AUC, a ranking measure, cannot rise. AUC is not the appropriate metric for selective prediction.
  • Reliability gain reported on the correct metric. Balanced accuracy at a calibrated operating point improves for all four classes at 30% rejection (NIH, bootstrap, all CIs exclude 0): Cardiomegaly +0.082 [0.056, 0.113], Effusion +0.043 [0.029, 0.060], Edema +0.086 [0.054, 0.125], Consolidation +0.076 [0.057, 0.095]. Replicated on MIMIC-CXR (three of four significant; fourth positive) and on an independently trained weighted-BCE model.
  • Borderline Grad-CAM added (representative confident-accepted vs borderline-deferred examples; accepted predictions concentrate activation over the lung fields, deferred predictions are more diffuse), alongside the gradient-volatility figure.
  • Reproducibility appendix: fixed seeds, input resolution (224×224) and normalization, patient-level calibration/evaluation split sizes, operating-threshold selection, optimizer/schedule, and a threshold-sensitivity analysis (0.70–0.99). Code and frozen per-image predictions released.
